## 1. Objective
What is this product for? The core objective in plain, specific terms — not a slogan.

## 2. Target audience
Who is this for? Primary and secondary users, their context, and the job they're hiring the product
to do. Be specific (avoid "everyone").

## 3. Competitors & their features
Who already solves this (or adjacent) problem, and how? For each competitor: what they do well, what
they do badly, and the key features they offer.

|------------|--------------|--------------|-----------|-------------------|
| | | | | |

> Research note: if competitor knowledge is incomplete or may be out of date, the best available model
> should research and populate this — mark each researched row with its source and date.

## 4. USP — the product's unique selling proposition
Given the audience and the competitor gaps above, what makes THIS product distinct and worth choosing?
The USP must follow logically from sections 2–3 (it answers "why us, not them?"), not be asserted.
Where derived by research/model reasoning, show the reasoning briefly.

The must-have features for v1. **Every feature carries its own objective** (the "why this feature
exists" — see the feature table below). Features are discussed and finalised in brainstorming.

> Each feature's objective is recorded here AND, if the feature grows large, in its own section of
> appflow.md / schema.md. A feature with no clear objective should be challenged, not built.

## 6. Success criteria
How do we know the product works and is worth building? Tie back to the objective (section 1).

## 7. Out of scope
What we are deliberately NOT building yet, and why.
